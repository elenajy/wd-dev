Methods to solve quadratic equations.

Quadratic Formula

The Quadratic formula can solve any quadratic equation.

  1. First, find the a, b, and c value. They are in the form of ax2 + bx + c.
  2. Then, square the b term.
  3. Multiply the a term by the c term and then the product by 4.
  4. Subtract your result from part 3 from your result in part 2.
  5. Take the square root of you previous answer.
  6. Add your answer to -b, and then also subtract is form -b. These two answers divided by 2 give you your two roots!.


Completing the Square

  1. Put the X terms on one side and the constants on the other side.
  2. Take half of the X term (coefficient), and square it.
  3. Rewrite the part in step 2 as a perfect square.
  4. Take the square root of both sides.
  5. Simplify the radical.
  6. Isolate the X term.
  7. Combine like terms.
  8. Solve for whatever makes it zero. These are your two roots.

Factoring

  1. Find whatever two numbers that add together to be the coefficient of the x term, and mulitply to be the constant.
  2. Rewrite it as (x+whatever term you found in part 1)*(x+the second term you found in part one.)
